An interior design taken from a notebook sketch to a full 3D render — a real room in the family home, designed to be a studio for making anything.

It started as a rough pencil sketch. The key tenets were **Motion, Modularity and Manipulation**: a space that could be reconfigured easily and effortlessly for whatever was being created that week.

The full space was then modelled and rendered to scale in Fusion 360 — walls, lighting, and furniture. Modular shelving mounts on French cleats so the whole wall can be rearranged without tools, and a dual-desk setup with dedicated 3D-printer shelving anchors the working side of the room.

The design document was shared with the design team for the actual build-out.
